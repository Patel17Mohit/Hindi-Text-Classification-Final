🧠 Language Models

Language Models enable computers to understand, predict, and generate natural language by learning patterns from large collections of text. They play a crucial role in modern Natural Language Processing, including Hindi Text Classification.

Concepts Covered

  1. Statistical Language Models
  2. Neural Language Models
  3. Transformer-Based Representations

19. Statistical Language Models

Definition

Statistical Language Models predict the probability of a word occurring based on the occurrence of previous words using statistical methods.

Purpose

Estimate the likelihood of word sequences for language understanding and text generation.

Working Principle

These models calculate probabilities from large text corpora. The probability of the next word depends on previously observed words.

Example

N-Gram Models

For the phrase:

"भारत ने विश्व..."

the model predicts the most probable next word using word frequency statistics.

Advantages

  • Simple to understand
  • Easy to implement
  • Interpretable probability calculations

Limitations

  • Limited context understanding
  • Cannot capture long-range dependencies
  • Performance decreases for large vocabularies

Applications

  • Spell Checking
  • Speech Recognition
  • Basic Text Prediction
  • Machine Translation

20. Neural Language Models

Definition

Neural Language Models use Artificial Neural Networks to learn complex relationships between words and sentences.

Purpose

Generate richer language representations and improve prediction accuracy compared to statistical methods.

Working Principle

Words are converted into dense vectors (embeddings) and processed through neural networks to learn contextual relationships.

Example

Instead of memorizing word frequencies, the model learns semantic similarities between words such as:

राजा   ⇄   रानी

Advantages

  • Better understanding of context
  • Captures semantic relationships
  • Higher prediction accuracy

Limitations

  • Requires large datasets
  • Needs significant computational resources
  • Longer training time

Applications

  • Text Classification
  • Machine Translation
  • Chatbots
  • Recommendation Systems

21. Transformer-Based Representations

Definition

Transformer-based models use the Self-Attention mechanism to understand relationships between all words in a sentence simultaneously, allowing better contextual understanding.

Purpose

Capture long-range dependencies and generate highly accurate context-aware language representations.

Working Principle

Instead of reading words one by one, transformers analyze the entire sentence at once and determine which words are most relevant to each other.

Popular Models

  • BERT
  • GPT
  • T5
  • IndicBERT
  • MuRIL

Example

The word "बैंक" has multiple meanings. Transformer models determine the correct meaning using surrounding words.

नदी का बैंक → River Bank

स्टेट बैंक → Financial Bank

Advantages

  • State-of-the-art performance
  • Context-aware understanding
  • Excellent semantic representation
  • Supports multilingual tasks including Hindi

Limitations

  • Computationally expensive
  • Requires GPUs/TPUs for efficient training
  • Large memory requirements

Applications

  • ChatGPT
  • Machine Translation
  • Hindi Text Classification
  • Question Answering
  • Search Engines
  • Text Summarization
