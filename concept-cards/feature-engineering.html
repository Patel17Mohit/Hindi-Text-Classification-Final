📊 Feature Engineering

Feature Engineering converts processed Hindi text into numerical representations that Machine Learning algorithms can understand and use for classification.

Concepts Covered

  1. Bag of Words (BoW)
  2. N-Grams
  3. Term Frequency (TF)
  4. Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)
  5. TF-IDF

9. Bag of Words (BoW)

Definition

Bag of Words (BoW) is a feature engineering technique that represents text using the frequency of words appearing in a document.

Purpose

To convert textual data into numerical vectors that Machine Learning models can process.

Example

Sentence

भारत क्रिकेट मैच जीता

Vector Representation

[भारत = 1, क्रिकेट = 1, मैच = 1, जीता = 1]

Advantages

  • Simple implementation
  • Easy to understand
  • Effective baseline model

Limitations

  • Ignores word order
  • Does not capture context or semantics

Applications

  • Spam Detection
  • News Classification
  • Document Classification

10. N-Grams

Definition

N-Grams are sequences of N consecutive words that preserve local word order and context.

Purpose

To capture relationships between neighboring words that are ignored by Bag of Words.

Example

Sentence

भारत ने मैच जीता

Bigrams

भारत ने
ने मैच
मैच जीता

Advantages

  • Captures local context
  • Improves classification accuracy

Limitations

  • Produces high-dimensional feature vectors
  • Requires more storage

Applications

  • Language Modeling
  • Text Classification
  • Machine Translation

11. Term Frequency (TF)

Definition

Term Frequency (TF) measures how frequently a word appears in a document.

Purpose

To identify words that are important within a particular document.

Example

If the word "क्रिकेट" appears 10 times in a sports article, its TF value is high.

Advantages

  • Easy to calculate
  • Reflects word importance within a document

Limitations

  • Frequently occurring words receive high scores even if they are common.

Applications

  • Search Systems
  • Document Ranking
  • Information Retrieval

12. Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

Definition

Inverse Document Frequency (IDF) measures how unique or informative a word is across an entire collection of documents.

Purpose

Reduce the importance of very common words while highlighting informative terms.

Example

The word "क्रिकेट" mainly appears in sports articles, therefore it receives a higher IDF score.

Advantages

  • Identifies informative words
  • Improves feature quality

Limitations

  • Very rare words may receive excessive importance.

Applications

  • Search Engines
  • Information Retrieval
  • Document Ranking

13. TF-IDF

Definition

TF-IDF combines Term Frequency (TF) and Inverse Document Frequency (IDF) to determine the importance of a word in a document relative to the entire collection.

Formula

TF-IDF = TF × IDF

Purpose

Assign higher weights to important words while reducing the influence of common words.

Advantages

  • Provides better feature representation
  • Reduces the effect of common words
  • Widely used in NLP applications

Limitations

  • Does not capture semantic meaning or word context.

Applications

  • Search Ranking
  • News Classification
  • Document Classification
  • Information Retrieval
