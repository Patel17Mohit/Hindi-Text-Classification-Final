📊 TF vs TF-IDF

Compare two important feature engineering techniques used to convert text into numerical representations for Natural Language Processing.

📖 Overview

Term Frequency (TF) and Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) are popular feature engineering techniques used to represent textual data numerically.

Both methods measure the importance of words in a document, but TF-IDF provides a more informative weighting mechanism by considering both word frequency and uniqueness across a collection of documents.

📊 Comparison Table

Parameter TF (Term Frequency) TF-IDF
Definition Counts how often a word appears in a document. Combines word frequency with rarity across documents.
Working Mechanism Uses only local document frequency. Uses both local and global document frequency.
Importance Measurement Frequency-based. Frequency and uniqueness-based.
Common Word Handling Common words receive high importance. Common words receive lower importance.
Computational Complexity Low Moderate
Accuracy Moderate High
Feature Quality Basic numerical representation. More informative feature representation.
Noise Reduction Poor Better
Suitability for Classification Acceptable Highly suitable

💡 Example

Consider two document categories:

🏏 Sports Articles

  • क्रिकेट
  • बल्लेबाज
  • विकेट

🏛 Politics Articles

  • चुनाव
  • संसद
  • मंत्री

Using TF, frequently occurring words receive high scores, even if they appear in many documents.

Using TF-IDF, category-specific words such as "क्रिकेट" and "चुनाव" receive higher weights because they are frequent within their category but rare across other categories.

✅ Strengths of TF

  • Easy to calculate.
  • Computationally efficient.
  • Suitable for basic text analysis.
  • Simple implementation.

❌ Weaknesses of TF

  • Cannot distinguish informative and non-informative words.
  • Common words may dominate feature values.
  • Less effective for classification.

✅ Strengths of TF-IDF

  • Highlights meaningful terms.
  • Improves classification performance.
  • Reduces influence of common words.
  • Produces better feature vectors.

❌ Weaknesses of TF-IDF

  • Does not capture semantic meaning.
  • Ignores word order.
  • Cannot understand context.

📌 Conclusion

For Hindi Text Classification, TF-IDF is generally preferred over Term Frequency (TF) because it identifies category-specific words more effectively.

Although TF is simple and computationally efficient, TF-IDF produces richer numerical representations and usually improves classification accuracy by assigning greater importance to informative words.
