🤖 Word2Vec vs FastText

Compare two popular word embedding techniques used to represent words as dense numerical vectors in Natural Language Processing.

📖 Overview

Word2Vec and FastText are word embedding techniques that convert words into dense numerical vectors. Both methods capture semantic relationships between words, but FastText extends Word2Vec by learning representations from character-level subword information.

This makes FastText especially effective for morphologically rich languages such as Hindi, where many words share common prefixes, suffixes, and root forms.

Word2Vec treats the entire word as one complete unit.

FastText divides the word into smaller character-level subwords (n-grams) and learns representations for each component.

Because of this approach, FastText can successfully generate embeddings for previously unseen or rare Hindi words.

✅ Strengths of Word2Vec

  • Efficient training.
  • Good semantic representations.
  • Lower memory consumption.
  • Widely used in NLP applications.

❌ Weaknesses of Word2Vec

  • Poor handling of rare words.
  • Cannot effectively represent unknown words.
  • Limited morphological understanding.

✅ Strengths of FastText

  • Handles unseen words effectively.
  • Uses character-level information.
  • Works well for morphologically rich languages.
  • Excellent for Hindi and other Indian languages.

❌ Weaknesses of FastText

  • Larger model size.
  • Slightly slower training.
  • Requires more memory.

🔑 Key Takeaway

The major difference is that Word2Vec learns embeddings for complete words, whereas FastText learns embeddings using character-level subwords.

This allows FastText to better understand rare words, misspellings, and the rich morphology of Hindi, making it more suitable for Hindi Text Classification.

📌 Conclusion

For Hindi Text Classification, FastText is generally preferred because Hindi contains many word variations, prefixes, and suffixes.

Its ability to learn subword representations makes it more robust, improves handling of unseen words, and often leads to better classification performance than Word2Vec.
